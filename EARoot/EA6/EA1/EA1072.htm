The IDEAS ontology makes a distinction between things and the ways in which they are represented (e.g. their names, identifiers, etc.). This may seem like an obvious distinction, but most software systems do not do this - i.e. they conflate the object and its name. <br /></p><p><br /></p><p>By separating representation from extent, IDEAS allows any given Thing to have more than one representation. For example, the set of all tables might be called "Table" and also called "Tisch". A given car could be identified both by its VIN number and its manufacturer serial number (note that the vehicle registration number only identifies a state of the vehicle from when it is manufactured to when it is unregistered).<br /></p><p><br /></p><p>This allows IDEAS to be very precise about the extent of things whilst still allowing a great deal of flexibility in representation.<br /></p>
